Units of real quadratic rings

The fundamental unit of a real quadratic integer ring is the smallest unit greater than 1. All other units can be obtained by a combination of powers, conjugates and negation.

These results were obtained with my Algebraic Integer Calculator program (see the GitHub repository). The program is limited to quadratic integers with real and imaginary parts that can be represented by 32-bit integers. It didn't seem like a problem for drawing diagrams of prime numbers in imaginary quadratic rings, since, without the ability to drag the diagram, even on an 8K display there is no risk of overflow.

However, for the task of finding the units of real quadratic rings, this limitation quickly becomes a serious problem. Going in ascending order by d, this first manifests itself for ℤ[√199]. Rather than expand the type or seek the answers for those affected domains elsewhere, I have simply put in the exception messages.
