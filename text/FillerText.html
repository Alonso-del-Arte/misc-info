Filler Text

Filler text. This is filler text. It does not com­mu­ni­cate anything par­tic­u­lar­ly im­por­tant or insightful, but it should at least be gram­mat­i­cal­ly cor­rect. I be­lieve that filler text should be in the same lan­guage as the larger text where it is to function as a placeholder.

Generally, filler text should not con­tain statements that are provably true or provably false. That can be ac­com­plished through vagueness, by with­hold­ing the necessary context to ascertain the veracity of a given state­ment.

Or, alternatively, filler text can con­tain statements that are provably true, but trivially so. Such as, for example, that true is the opposite of false and false is the opposite of true.

In the past, some looked ahead to the future with hope and optimism. And in the future, some look back to the past with nostalgia. For each day, except maybe the very first day and the very last day, there is a yesterday and a tomorrow to today.
